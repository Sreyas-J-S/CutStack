Printing & Cutting Guide

Creating the imposed PDF file is only half the battle. To get professional results, you need to print and cut it correctly. Here is our step-by-step guide.

1. Printing Setup

When you open your imposed PDF in Adobe Acrobat or your browser's print dialog:

  • Scale: Always select "Actual Size" or "Scale 100%". Do not select "Fit to Page," or your margins will be incorrect.
  • Two-Sided (Duplex): Select "Print on both sides".
  • Flip Edge: Usually, you want to "Flip on Long Edge" (standard book orientation).

2. Handling Alignment Issues

Home printers naturally have a "mechanical shift"—the paper might shift 1-2mm as it travels through the rollers. This creates slight misalignment between the front and back sides.

Tips to minimize this:

  • Use thicker paper (24lb or 32lb stock) which feeds more consistently.
  • In CutStack, use the "Print-Safe" layout mode, which adds generous margins between cards, making slight cuts less noticeable.

3. The Cutting Process

Once you have your stack of printed sheets:

  1. Keep them stacked: Do not shuffle the sheets. Keep them in the order they came out of the printer.
  2. Cut Columns First: Use a guillotine paper cutter. Cut along the vertical center lines first.
  3. Stack Columns: Take the right-hand stack and place it under the left-hand stack (or vice versa, depending on the imposition order—check your page numbers).
  4. Cut Rows: Now cut the horizontal lines of your combined stack.

If done correctly, you will have a perfectly ordered deck of cards or pages!
