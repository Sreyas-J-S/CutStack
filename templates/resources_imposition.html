What is N-Up Imposition?

If you've ever tried to print business cards or flyers at home, you know the struggle: you print one page, then another, and you waste a lot of paper. Professional printers solve this with a technique called Imposition.

The Basics of "N-Up"

"N-Up" simply refers to placing N number of pages on a single side of a sheet of paper.

  • 2-Up: Two pages per side (common for half-sheet flyers).
  • 4-Up: Four pages per side (often used for postcards).
  • 10-Up: Ten pages per side (standard business card layout).

Why Use Imposition?

  1. Save Paper: Printing multiple items on a single large sheet drastically reduces waste.
  2. Save Time: Printing 100 business cards 10-Up only requires 10 sheets of paper, versus 100 sheets.
  3. Professional Finish: Proper imposition adds "bleed" and crop marks, ensuring your final cut looks clean and edge-to-edge.

The "Cut & Stack" Method

This is where CutStack shines. Most simple imposition tools just repeat the same page 4 times on a sheet. That's fine for a single flyer, but what if you have a 100-page PDF document?

If you use standard "step-and-repeat," you'll end up with 4 piles of unsorted pages. You would have to manually sort them: A1, B1, C1, D1... it's a nightmare.

Cut & Stack (or Stack Imposition) arranges the pages intelligently. It calculates the total number of sheets needed and places pages so that when you cut the stack vertically:

  • The first stack contains pages 1-25.
  • The second stack contains pages 26-50.
  • The third stack contains pages 51-75.
  • The fourth stack contains pages 76-100.

You literally just stack the piles on top of each other, and your entire document is in order. Zero manual sorting required.
